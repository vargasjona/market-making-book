### How to read this book

<p>This book is organized like a course, in the spirit of <em>The Rust Programming Language</em>: each chapter builds on the previous one, and the navigation lives in the sidebar. <strong>Part I</strong> teaches trading from absolute zero — the order book, then the chart toolkit every platform shares (candlesticks, timeframes, EMAs, order types), then the market maker's role and history. <strong>Part II</strong> develops the theory — the real mathematics, but every formula arrives with a parable that makes it obvious. <strong>Part III</strong> dissects six real venues. <strong>Part IV</strong> matches algorithms to asset classes, covers risk, and closes with a hands-on crypto practicum — a complete, reproducible path from signal research to a live bot. <strong>Part V</strong> proposes six new, testable, state-of-the-art strategies — the destination of the whole book.</p>

<p>Every dark panel is a <strong>live figure</strong>: drag the sliders, press the buttons, break things. The figures are simplified simulations designed to build intuition, not production simulators.</p>

<div className="keyidea"><span className="tag">The one-sentence summary</span>Market making is the business of selling immediacy: you quote two prices at once, earn the gap between them, and spend your entire professional life managing the two risks that gap must pay for — <strong>inventory risk</strong> and <strong>adverse selection</strong>.</div>

<p className="small">Educational material, not financial advice. Fee schedules and venue rules change frequently — always verify against official documentation (Chapter 19 collects the references).</p>
